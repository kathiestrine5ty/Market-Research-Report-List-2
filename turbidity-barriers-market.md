<p><p>The turbidity barriers market is categorized into woven and non-woven fibers, each serving distinct purposes in water management. Woven fiber barriers are composed of tightly interlaced materials, providing enhanced strength and durability, making them suitable for high-flow environments. In contrast, non-woven fiber barriers utilize a network of fibers that are bonded together, allowing for more flexibility and easier deployment in various conditions. Both types effectively control sediment and contaminants, ensuring water clarity and environmental protection in construction and remediation projects.</p></p>
