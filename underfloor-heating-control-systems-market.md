<p><p>Underfloor heating control systems can be categorized into two main types: electric and hydraulic. Electric systems use electrical cables or mats to generate heat, providing quick installation and precise control, making them suitable for smaller areas. In contrast, hydraulic systems employ water heated by a boiler, circulated through pipes beneath the floor, offering energy efficiency and consistent warmth across larger spaces. Both systems enhance comfort and energy efficiency, tailored to different building types and user preferences.</p></p>
